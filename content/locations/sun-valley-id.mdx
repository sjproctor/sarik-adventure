  The first stop on our adventure is Sun Valley in central Idaho. Nestled next to Ketchum in the Wood River Valley, Sun Valley is known for skiing and celebrity second homes.
  It's a popping place during peak winter and summer but our residency falls during what the locals call "slack." Unlike the Slack in my life, this one is very chill. We practically have the place to ourselves. It’s like the covid era minus facemasks and the perpetual fear of death.
  This might be the best time of year to visit Sun Valley. Don't tell the locals I said that.
overview: |
  Sun Valley was built in 1936 as the first winter resort next to the mountain town of Ketchum in Central Idaho. It's shockingly beautiful with velvety rolling hills and streets lined with pines and shimmering quaking aspens.

    - Bald Mountain (aka Baldy) in the background of everything. The 9,150 foot summit creates an iconic backdrop.
    - [Pioneer Saloon](https://www.pioneersaloon.com/) is an obvious pick but one that cannot be missed. Baked potatoes the size of your head.
    - The papaya salad at [Rickshaw](https://eat-at-rickshaw.com/) is unique and fantastic.
    - The [Mountain Rides](https://mountainrides.org/) bus system here is A+. There is a route through Ketchum and Sun Valley as well as airport shuttles, lines to neighboring towns, and additional routes in peak seasons. All for free. Plus their website highlights the next stop in real time.
    - If you get here before June the [White Clouds, Trail Creek, and Elkhorn](https://www.sunvalley.com/things-to-do/golf/) golf courses have off-season pricing. Nice if you aren't up for selling a kidney.
    - [Merlin's Magic Lantern](https://merlinsmagiclantern.com/homepage) movie theater in Ketchum offers half price tickets on Mondays. Plus they sell beer and wine.
    - FRXSKIHQ is a whole vibe. The staff is personable and kooky in the best way. Get a $5 chicken sandwich and a $1 PBR and take in the ski shack meets dorm room decor.

    _If You Are The Type Of Person Who Has Any Interest In These Types Of Things_

    In the early 80's my parents move to Ketchum when my dad took a job at the [Idaho Mountain Express](https://www.mtexpress.com/) weekly newspaper. My parents married at the Church of the Big Wood by the river and a few years later I was born at Mortiz hospital near the Sun Valley Lodge. We moved to Boise when I was little and I haven't spent much time in the area since.

    In the mid 1800s, the Wood River Valley in the Sawtooth mountains of central Idaho was found to have a silver lode. Prospectors brought industry which brought the railroad to an area near the base of Bald Mountain named Ketchum known for its majestic views and a natural hot springs reported to heal what ails ya.

    Towards the end of the Great Depression, a nepo baby chairman of Union Pacific Railroad decided to develop a destination winter resort as a way to promote the use of the railroad in the western US. Hoping capitalize on the popularity of alpine skiing after the Olympics in Lake Placid, W. Averell Harriman liked the sunny skies and consistent snowfall of the Ketchum area. Dollar and Proctor Mountains were chosen for the ski runs and a lodge was build on land purchased from a family of cattle and sheep ranchers.

    During construction of the lodge, Union Pacific commissioned an engineer to create a means of transporting skiers to the top of the mountain that would improve on the European system called a J-bar which wasn't much more than a rope tow with a long handle. The first ariel chairlifts were installed Proctor Mountain in 1936. In 1939, three additional lifts were installed at the much larger Bald Mountain which offered skiers steeper slopes and longer runs.

    W. Averell Harriman promoted his new resort by inviting celebrities to visit.
