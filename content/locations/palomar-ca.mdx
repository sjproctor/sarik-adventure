      The observatory is an active astronomical research center operated by Caltech. It houses the Hale telescope which uses a 200-inch Pyrex parabolic glass plate to focus light from deep space which can be studied by capturing images (originally on glass plates, now digitally) or by analyzing the wavelengths (called spectroscopy). Edwin Hubble took the first image on January 26, 1949 of the a nebula named for him.
